Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It has an average radius of about nine times that of Earth. Satrun's interior is composed of rock, surrouded by layers of hydrogen, helium and gas. It's rings are composed of mainly ice, but also rocky debris and dust. 146 moons have been discovered orbiting the planet.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It's diameter is eleven times that of Earth. Jupiter contains trace elements and compounds like carbon, oxygen, sulfur, neon, ammonia, water vapour, phosphine, hydrogen sulfide, and hydrocarbons. It is the third-brightest natural object in the Earth's night sky.
